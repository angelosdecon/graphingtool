Utility Function

A utility function represents a consumer's preferences over two goods x and y. The Cobb-Douglas form is one of the most widely used in economics.

U(x, y) = xα · y(1−α)

The partial derivatives give us marginal utility — the extra satisfaction from one more unit of each good.

Production Function

A Cobb-Douglas production function describes how capital (K) and labour (L) combine to produce output (Q).

Q(K, L) = Kα · L(1−α)

The partial derivatives give us marginal products — the additional output from one more unit of each input.

What is a Derivative?

A derivative measures how fast a function changes at a particular point. Think of driving — the derivative is your speedometer reading right now, not your average speed for the whole trip.

Formally, the derivative of f(x) at a point x₀ is the slope of the tangent line to the curve:

f'(x) = limΔx→0 [f(x+Δx) − f(x)] / Δx

In economics, derivatives appear everywhere: marginal cost, marginal revenue, and marginal utility are all derivatives.

Example: If total cost is C(q) = q², then marginal cost = C'(q) = 2q. At q = 3, marginal cost = 6.

What is a Partial Derivative?

When a function depends on more than one variable, a partial derivative measures how the function changes when only one variable changes — holding all others fixed.

Think of a 3D landscape. ∂f/∂x is the steepness if you walk east. ∂f/∂y is the steepness if you walk north.

∂f/∂x = rate of change of f as x changes, y held fixed

Economics example: If U(x,y) = x·y, then ∂U/∂x = y — the marginal utility of good x depends on how much of good y you already have.

What is a Differential?

The differential is a linear approximation of how much a function changes when you move a small amount in any direction. Instead of the curved surface, you use the flat tangent plane.

df ≈ (∂f/∂x)·dx + (∂f/∂y)·dy

This works well for small changes — the tangent plane hugs the surface near the point of contact, but diverges further away.

Economics example: If utility depends on two goods and prices change slightly, the differential tells you the approximate change in utility without recalculating from scratch.
